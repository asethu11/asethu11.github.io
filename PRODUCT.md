# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Recruiters, hiring managers, and design/product peers evaluating Abhishek Sethuraman as a UX & Product Designer. They arrive to judge craft and credibility quickly, often skimming on the way to deciding whether to reach out.

## Product Purpose
A personal portfolio for a UX/Product designer focused on AI-native products (LLMs, RAG, agentic workflows) and enterprise experiences at scale (220K+ users). It exists to demonstrate, not just claim, design judgment through case studies, writing, and the craft of the site itself. Success is a visitor concluding "this person can design and build" and following through (LinkedIn, email, a case study read).

## Brand Personality
Confident, precise, and human, with dry wit ("uh-bee-shake", "Your LinkedIn just called, said it needs me"). The site wears its hand-built nature as identity: a sticky "site coded by a human in vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript" badge and a matching footer line. Voice is plainspoken and specific, never corporate.

## Anti-references
AI-generated portfolio templates and the generic SaaS-cream landing page. The explicit anti-reference is "looks like AI made it", which the human-coded badge stakes out directly. Also: theme-y over-rounded ghost cards, gradient-text headlines, decorative glassmorphism, and per-section uppercase eyebrows.

## Design Principles
- Practice what you preach: a UX designer's own site is the first case study; craft is the argument.
- Show, don't tell: prove ability with the artifact, not adjectives.
- Human over template: distinctive, opinionated touches over safe defaults.
- Constraint as system: two typefaces, a single gray ramp, an 8px rhythm, purpose-named tokens.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Theme-aware (light/dark, manual + system), aria-labels on icon links, reduced-motion handling in motion.css. Maintain WCAG AA contrast for body and metadata text in both themes.
