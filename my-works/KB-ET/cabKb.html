Knowledge Base Experience Research

As CreateAI Builder (CAB) matured, one feature became its backbone: the Knowledge Base, where users upload documents, URLs, and data that power their AI projects by adding more context.

By mid-2025, it had also become the most complex and least understood part of the platform. Faculty and researchers loved its potential but struggled to trust or control it.

As a UX Researcher, I led a deep-dive into how users actually experienced the Knowledge Base — uncovering hidden friction, mapping mental-model gaps, and translating technical ambiguity into product direction.

5-7 min read | Oct '25 - Ongoing | UX Research (with Product & Engineering) | Usability, Error Prevention

TL;DR

Problem:

Uploads failed silently, settings were confusing, and users didn't trust what the system was doing.

Process:

Combined qualitative (User chat from Slack Channels, Support tickets, User Interviews, Office Hours Transcripts) and quantitative (Datamart, Tableau) data to uncover root causes and guide product direction.

Outcome:

Insights drove roadmap priorities for upload transparency, syncing, error clarity, and guided onboarding. Expected impact: 15-20% reduction in support tickets (Measuring over the next 3 months).

Key Findings

Transparency & Trust

  • Upload and retrieval status were invisible or misleading.
  • Eye-icon behavior confused users ("is it syncing again?").
  • Users couldn't tell which sources powered an answer.
"I don't know what the bot is doing with my knowledge."

Design Direction:

Add visible upload progress, file states (active/hidden/failed), and citation visibility.

Terminology & Cognitive Overload

  • Terms like Top K, Retrieval Type, and Behaviors alienated non-technical users.
  • Users couldn't distinguish chat uploads vs. Knowledge uploads.
"I don't understand the knobs and switches."

Design Direction:

Simplify defaults, use plain-English labels, and introduce contextual tooltips and examples.

Reliability of Ingested Content

  • PDFs parsed inconsistently; CSVs lost header relationships.
  • ASU URLs didn't refresh due to weekly iSearch crawl cycles.
  • Users thought toggling the eye icon re-synced data — it didn't.
"I don't trust that my files and links are being read correctly."

Design Direction:

Show "last updated" date, integrate Firecrawl for URL ingestion, add ingestion-quality feedback.

Upload & Setup Frustrations

  • Uploads failed silently.
  • Hidden and active states were indistinguishable.
  • Behavior toggles disabled KB without visual feedback.
"I don't know if my setup is correct."

Design Direction:

Introduce retry/delete actions, progress bar, and clearer separation between upload, enable, and reference states.

Learning & Independence

  • Faculty wanted autonomy, not hand-holding.
  • They needed lightweight guidance on how to tune and interpret results.
"I want to learn how to use this tool, not just get answers."

Design Direction:

Build in-product learning, "Knowledge Health" dashboards, and conversational debugging cues.

Quantitative Signals

(Visual placeholder for two charts: "Projects by # of uploads" and "Top error categories")

  • 4,087 total CAB projects analyzed
  • 1,976 used Knowledge Base (~50%)
  • 60% had 0–5 uploads → high early friction
  • 33% of all feedback related to upload failures
  • 20% of support tickets linked to Knowledge or Behaviors configuration

The Three Meta-Themes

Theme Meaning Design Implication
Transparency Users should see what the system is doing — uploads, retrievals, failures. Add progress and visibility layers.
Simplicity Replace developer language with professor-friendly defaults. Plain-English UI, simplified behaviors.
Guided Learning The tool should teach users how to use it. Progressive onboarding, contextual help, "debug with me" prompts.

(visual placeholder: diagram linking the three themes to CAB feature roadmap)

Impact

Direct roadmap influence across multiple releases:

  • File Upload Transparency (visible progress + retry/delete)
  • Firecrawl Integration for URL ingestion reliability
  • Auto-Sync & Download Capabilities for Docs and Drive
  • In-Product Learning & "Knowledge Health" concept approved for design
  • Reduced confusion in support channels by clarifying "Knowledge vs. Chat" behavior
  • Strengthened collaboration between Design, Eng, and PM through a shared evidence base
