Engagement context.

Point One Zero · Confidential 04 / 12
point one zero. Section 02 · Engagement context
02.1 · Brief

What Northwind asked for.

The Investment Office wanted to replace a 14-year-old investor portal that had become the bottleneck for every quarter-end reporting cycle. The system handled 240 accounts but required twelve people-hours of manual reconciliation per quarter, and the third-party vendor was charging $1.8M / yr to maintain code nobody on the team could read.

The internal team had already attempted two replatforms in the previous three years. Both stalled at the data-modeling phase. The brief that came to us was specific: "replace the portal, keep the data warehouse, ship something we can actually own."

Why this one shipped

Two earlier attempts treated the portal as a UI replatform. We treated it as a data-modeling exercise with a thin UI on top — the prototype shipped because the data layer was correct on day one, and the UI followed in days, not months.

02.2 · Scope & team

One Build pod (designer + two engineers + AI lead) for six weeks. Northwind embedded their head of data and one platform engineer. We worked from the same Linear board, the same Figma file, the same staging environment from day one.

point one zero. Section 03 · What we shipped
03.2 · Operational change

Before / after the engagement.

The quarter-end reconciliation cycle that used to consume twelve people-hours per quarter now completes in under thirty minutes. The 47 reusable AI agents we authored handle the reconciliation, the audit trail, and the reporting deck — Northwind's team supervises, signs off, and ships.

Before

Quarterly cycle · 12 ppl-hrs

Manual reconciliation across three systems. Vendor-supplied PDFs. Audit trail in spreadsheet form. Reporting deck assembled from scratch every quarter.

After

Quarterly cycle · 30 mins

Reconciliation runs on schedule via the agent registry. Audit trail is automatic and queryable. Reporting deck regenerates from the data — humans review, sign, and ship.

Handoff note

The 47 agents stay in production after we leave. Each is narrow, well-tested, authored once. Northwind's platform engineer pairs on every agent during build week — by handoff, the team reads and modifies the agent registry as easily as the rest of the codebase.

Section 05

Three moves for the next quarter.

Each recommendation is bounded — scope, owner, time-frame. The Investment Office reviews quarterly; nothing here requires a board decision.

01

Extend the agent registry to LP reporting.

The same agents reconcile portfolio data; extending to the quarterly LP letter is a two-sprint exercise. Estimated 60% reduction in time-to-letter.

Owner · Northwind platform
02

Add a read-only investor view.

240 accounts × four quarterly checkpoints = ~1,000 service interactions / yr. A read-only investor view cuts service load by an estimated 30–40%.

Owner · Point One Zero · 6 weeks
03

Stand up an internal "data team of two".

The current pattern is one engineer + one analyst. A two-person team with shared ownership of the agent registry would shorten the audit cycle further and reduce dependency on us for ongoing maintenance.

Owner · Northwind · ongoing

Appendix — methodology & references.

Where the numbers come from, what's measured, what isn't, and who to contact for the underlying detail.

A.1 · Methodology

Cost figures are sourced from Northwind's finance system, normalized to monthly run-rate. The 47% reduction is computed against a six-month pre-engagement baseline rolling average. Time-to-prototype is wall-clock hours from kickoff call to first runnable staging artifact.

A.2 · Scope & limitations

The figures exclude (a) one-time termination costs paid to the previous vendor, (b) Northwind's internal opportunity cost during the six-week engagement, and (c) third-party SaaS spend that was already contracted. A separate engagement-level P&L is available on request.
