AI-Powered Data Extraction Interface for Medical Researchers

We built an Airtable-style “smart spreadsheet” that lets doctors pull structured clinical trial data from PDFs—fast, transparently, and with full control. The tool blends AI-assisted extraction with human validation, streamlining a previously manual and error-prone workflow into a focused, fluid experience.

What Are Living Systematic Reviews?

Living Systematic Reviews (LSRs) are a modern evolution of traditional systematic reviews. Instead of publishing once and becoming outdated, LSRs are designed to be continuously updated as new research emerges. This makes them essential for fast-evolving fields like clinical medicine, public health, and COVID-era trials, where timely evidence can impact real‑world decisions.

But the process of maintaining an LSR is intensely manual:

  • Researchers must screen and extract data from dozens or hundreds of new studies regularly.
  • Even small updates can require repeating the entire review pipeline—screening, extraction, synthesis.

Our goal was to rethink this pipeline, specifically the data extraction bottleneck, by building a tool that doesn’t just speed up the task, but fundamentally supports the human judgment that underpins every evidence‑based decision.

Why It Mattered

  • Mountains of PDFs: Clinicians were manually keying data from 100 + study papers per review.
  • Error‑prone & exhausting: Copy‑paste mistakes slowed publication and sapped research budgets.
  • Goal: Replace scattered tools with one friction‑free workspace that blends AI suggestions with human oversight.

Design Wins

  • Smart Columns: Cells auto-suggest values; conflicts highlighted
  • Evidence Side Panel: Inline PDF snippet + AI model comparison
  • One-Click Resolution: Users confirm or override suggestions; model improves
  • Progress Heatmap: Visual momentum through color-coded row progress

Impact & Next Steps

In pilot tests with 3 researchers on 72 papers, extraction time dropped by ~4×. 4/5 of testers said they would use the tool in future reviews.

We're now building a high-fidelity prototype for grant submission and broader deployment by Q3 2025.
