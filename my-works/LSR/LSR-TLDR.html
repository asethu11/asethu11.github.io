AI-Powered Data Extraction Interface for Clinical Researchers

We built an Airtable-style “smart spreadsheet” that lets doctors pull structured clinical trial data from PDFs—fast, transparently, and with full control. The tool blends AI-assisted extraction with human validation, streamlining a previously manual and error-prone workflow into a focused, fluid experience.

TL;DR | View Project in Detail

The Problem Space

Clinical teams spend 1000+ hours manually extracting trial data, causing fatigue, costly errors, and delayed findings. A streamlined AI-driven process can cut weeks of work and improve research reliability.


Timeline

Mar–Apr 2025

Role

Product Designer & Team Lead

Tools

Figma, FigJam, ChatGPT

The Challenge

  • Data Deluge: Over 1000 PDFs per review cycle to parse and extract.
  • Error Risk: Manual copy-paste leads to transcription mistakes and budget overruns.
  • Tool Fragmentation: Switching between PDF viewers, spreadsheets, and search tools disrupts focus.
  • Trust Barrier: Clinicians skeptical of AI without transparent evidence links.

Our Approach

  1. Empathy & Context: Conducted ride‑alongs, shadowing clinicians in live research sessions to map real‑world workflows and pain points.
  2. Synthesis & Principles: Organized 50+ notes via affinity mapping to define five core design tenets: Familiarity, Transparency, Contextual Evidence, Efficiency, and Control.
  3. Iterative Prototyping: Ran four two‑week sprints—low‑fi sketches → interactive FigJam flows → Figma mockups with AI overlays → developer syncs to validate feasibility and performance.
  4. Collaborative Testing: Hosted usability sessions with 6 clinicians, gathering feedback on AI suggestion accuracy and UI clarity; iterated on priority conflicts and trust indicators.

Solution Highlights

  • Smart Columns: Inline AI suggestions + conflict flags
  • Evidence Panel: Side‑by‑side PDF excerpts
  • One‑Click Confirm: Human‑in‑loop safety switch
  • AI confidence scores: Vote‑based conflict identifiers that involve humans only when necessary.

Impact

  • 4× Speedup in data extraction
  • 80% Tester Adoption in prototype trials
  • Stakeholder buy‑in at Mayo Clinic & ASU

Next Steps

Building a high‑fidelity React prototype, integrating deeper LLM explainability, and running a 10‑user beta study to refine workflows.
