What Are Living Systematic Reviews?

Living Systematic Reviews (LSRs) are a modern evolution of traditional systematic reviews. Instead of publishing once and becoming outdated, LSRs are designed to be continuously updated as new research emerges. This makes them essential for fast-evolving fields like clinical medicine, public health, and COVID-era trials, where timely evidence can impact real‑world decisions.

The Human Cost of Manual Review

Every systematic review begins with a mountain of PDFs and ends with weary clinicians triple‑checking tables. We shadowed Dr. Khan and Dr. Umar as they copied sample sizes into Excel, toggling between windows, fingers dancing faster than his eyes could track. Midway through the tenth paper Dr. Khan sighed, "I open the PDF, scroll to the tables, copy the number, alt-tab to Excel. Over and over. For hours."

Multiply that by a hundred papers, and you see the issue: a process designed for machines, executed by humans.

Existing tools like Covidence, Rayyan, or DistillrSR failed on usability. Doctors either abandoned them or hacked their own makeshift systems. The field desperately needed a human-centered, AI-augmented solution, not just faster, but actually usable and trustworthy.
