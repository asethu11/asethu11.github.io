Reimagining Youtube Music

YouTube Music has the potential to be a dominant player in the streaming space, but its design, and UX hold it back. This redesign explores how small but impactful UI/UX changes could improve navigation, customization, and overall user satisfaction.

Why Youtube Music Falls Behind

Despite YouTube's massive ecosystem, YouTube Music holds less than 10% of the market.

Why? Maybe the design, limited user control, and some missed opportunities. To understand what users wanted, I explored online discussions, reviews, and personal frustrations with the app.

User feedback points to some glaring issues:

  • Navigation is confusing, with features buried under layers of clicks.
  • Playlists lack flexibility, limiting personalization.
  • Podcasts and other content types are hidden or hard to access.
  • Samples feel more like an afterthought than a compelling feature.

Reimagining the Experience

To address these pain points, I focused on four key improvements:

  • UI Simplification: Cleaning up the interface for smoother separation between videos and audio.
  • Layout Customization: Allowing users more control over playlists and layouts.
  • User Freedom: Empowering users to sort and organize content effortlessly.
  • Clutter Reduction: Making the app feel less crowded and more enjoyable to use.
